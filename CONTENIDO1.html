Battle of stalingrad

The Battle of Stalingrad was a war between the Red Army of the Soviet Union and the Wehrmacht of Nazi Germany and their Axis allies for control of the Soviet city of Stalingrad, present-day Volgograd, between August 23, 1942 and the February 2, 1943.
The Battle of Stalingrad is considered the bloodiest in the history of humanity. The serious defeat of Nazi Germany and its allies in this city meant a key point and a severe inflection in the final results of the war; representing the beginning of the end of Nazism in Europe, as the Wehrmacht would never regain its offensive capacity or obtain more strategic victories on the Eastern Front.

The German offensive to capture Stalingrad began in the late summer of 1942 as part of Operation Blue or Fall Blau, an attempt by Germany to seize the oil wells of the Caucasus.

The German defeat at Stalingrad confirmed what many military experts suspected: the logistical capacity of the German forces was insufficient to supply and sustain an offensive on a front that stretched from the Black Sea to the Arctic Ocean.
