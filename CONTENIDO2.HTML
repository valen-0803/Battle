Red army

The Red Army of Workers and Peasants RKKA, was the official denomination of the army and the air force of the Soviet Federative Socialist Republic of Russia and, after 1922, of the Soviet Union. The Army was established immediately after the October Revolution of 1917.
The Bolsheviks raised an army to oppose the Confederate military who were under the counterrevolutionary White Movement during the Russian Civil War.



Starting in February 1946, the Red Army, together with the Soviet Navy, would be the main component of the Soviet Armed Forces, taking the official name "Soviet Army" until the dissolution of the Soviet Union in December 1991.

The Red Army is credited as a decisive force in the Allied victory over the Axis powers in the European theater in World War II. During operations on the Eastern Front, the Red Army inflicted devastating damage on the forces of Nazi Germany, which deployed 80-83% of its forces, causing the most total casualties compared to other allied countries and ultimately capturing the German capital of Berlin after a battle in which approximately two hundred thousand people perished, between civilians and soldiers from both sides.
