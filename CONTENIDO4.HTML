Operation blue

Operation Blue was executed by the German Wehrmacht in 1942. The objective of this operation was to take away the oil wells of the Caucasus from the Soviet Union.
Image 1. Advance of the Axis forces, as part of Operation Blue
Background
On June 22, 1941, the German armies invaded the Soviet Union. The ability of the U.S.S.R. to create new divisions and the arrival of cold winter were reasons for the German defeat in the Battle of Moscow. Hitler began planning an offensive in southern Russia with the objetive of capturing the Caucasus, which was the one that provided oil to the Soviet Union.

OBJECTIVES OF THE HITLER PLAN
To carry out Hitler's plan, the German armies would have had to have double strength, triple mobility and quadruple aviation. The troops had had to rest and be completed.

They had fought relentlessly since the beginning of the war with the USSR, and the losses they had suffered had not been made up for in personnel or materiel. The companies rarely numbered more than 60 men, the Panzer divisions, 80 tanks.

On September 9, Hitler takes direct command of Group A and completely loses interest in the Caucasus, focusing his gaze on Stalingrad. Meanwhile, 20 divisions of the Axis forces fought for every meter advancing into Stalingrad, and the Luftwaffe bombardments of the city only created more chaos which the Soviet defenders used to better hide.

FAILURE OF THE BLUE OPERATION
Hitler's obsession with taking Stalingrad caused him to lose sight of the initial objective of Operation Blue: the capture of the oil wells of the Caucasus.
