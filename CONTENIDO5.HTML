The uranium operation

On November 2, STAVKA, the high command of the Red Army, will prepare Operation Uranus aimed at pushing the Germans in the Don region westward, encircling the German 6th Army in Stalingrad, where by the way today the 151 and 152 Soviet Brigades are launching a successful counterattack to relieve the German pressure on the city.

Operation Uranus (in Russian: Операция Уран) was a military operation executed by the Soviet Union to liberate the city of Stalingrad from German troops during World War II. Its final objective was the expulsion of German forces from the Volga River, trapping those located in Stalingrad. The offensive was launched on November 19, 1942 and the two independent forces that were to surround Stalingrad met four days later.
