Final surrenders

In Stalingrad there is violent fighting between Russians and Germans; the troops of the 6th Army are decimated, exhausted, suffering from the cold and diseases. The lack of food has led the besieged to eat about 12,000 horses. Seven Soviet armies, under the command of Zhukov, are closing in on Stalingrad and pressing inland to annihilate the defenders; because of their precarious air supply, from tomorrow their daily ration of bread will drop from 200 to 100 grams. Paulus, disgusted at the absurdity of Hitler's orders, realized that, for the F端hrer, the 6th Army, or what was left of it, was little less than a sacrificial piece in the game of war. The lives of the soldiers were not of the least importance to Hitler. Because while Nazi hierarchs like Erich Koch, the Gauleiter or governor of the occupied territories of Ukraine, chartered a Luftwaffe plane to Rostov to bring him 200 pounds of caviar, while soldiers starved or died of starvation in Stalingrad. Nazi hierarchs will ask for his cessation; but the Reich is infested with these corrupt politicians. The F端hrer defends them by their bloodthirsty and efficient capacity to exploit resources and necessary labor for the war. The civilians of the occupied territories hate them.59 By December 25th, in the Kessel, 1280 soldiers die of cold and hunger. For the new year, the Soviets set up a series of kitchens and held parties on the south bank of the Volga with the double aim of celebrating the year and demoralizing the encircled Germans.

At the end of the month, the 4th Romanian Army was annihilated during the counteroffensive of the Red Army towards Kotielnovski by Malinovsky's 2nd Army of Guards, what is left of the 4th Panzerarmee is retreating to 200-240 km from Stalingrad. Operation Winter Storm was rejected. The Russian forces of the Stalingrad Front arrive at the Verkhne-Rubezhni-Tormosin-Gluboki line, having the possibility of launching a big offensive on the southern sector of the German front.

At 6:05 a.m. on January 10, the high command of the Stalingrad Front gives the order to attack the German positions in Stalingrad. Operation Koltso or Ring began with the firing of some 7,000 Katyuska guns, mortars and rocket launchers that for 55 minutes beat the German trenches. Then, waves of infants supported by tanks are launched. The offensive is centered on taking the Pitomnik airfield, where the Ju 52 land, bringing supplies to the besieged and carrying away their wounded. That day, the F端hrer radiates to Von Paulus "I forbid the capitulation. The troops must defend their positions to the last man and the last cartridge, so that with their heroic behavior they can contribute to the stabilization of the front and the defense of the West.

By February 2, the 51st Army Corps, under the command of General Streker or Schrenck, will surrender. With this action the combat for the devastated city ends, the biggest battle of the Second World War. From January 10 until today, the Red Army has eliminated 22 divisions of the Werhmacht, and another 160 units sent to help the 6th Army. Some 11,000 German soldiers did not surrender and continued to fight until the end; at the beginning of March the Soviets destroyed the last pockets of resistance in the basements and tunnels.
